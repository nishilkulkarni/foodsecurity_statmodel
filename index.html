Does the Financialization of Agricultural Commodities Impact Food Security?

By Manogna R. L. and Nishil Kulkarni

Abstract

The financialization of agricultural commodities has significant implications for food security. This study examines the impacts on developed and developing countries, focusing on wheat, maize, and soybeans.

Key Highlights

  • Financialization has a greater negative impact on developing nations.
  • Monetary policy moderates the effect of financialization on food security.
  • Wheat and soybean futures have the most pronounced influence on volatility.
